Exploring the depths of Quantum Mechanics and Computation

Introduction

Quantum computing has revolutionized the way we think about computational power. It leverages quantum mechanics to perform computations far beyond the capabilities of classical computers.

Understanding Qubits

A qubit can exist in a superposition of states, unlike classical bits.

Mathematical Representation

In quantum mechanics, a qubit is represented as a linear combination of basis states:

$$ |\psi\rangle = \alpha |0\rangle + \beta |1\rangle $$

where \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) are complex numbers satisfying \( |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 \).

Quantum Algorithms

One of the most famous quantum algorithms is Shor's algorithm, which can factorize large integers exponentially faster than classical algorithms [1].
