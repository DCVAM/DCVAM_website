Week 1: The Origins of Control Theory

Control theory has been a crucial part of engineering and automation since the early days of machinery. From ancient water clocks to modern drone stabilization systems, control systems help maintain stability and precision.

Early Examples of Control Systems

One of the earliest examples of feedback control was the **centrifugal governor**, developed by James Watt in the 18th century. This device automatically regulated steam engines by adjusting fuel input.

Mathematical Foundations

Control systems are often modeled using differential equations. The basic feedback loop can be represented as:

$$ G(s) = \frac{K}{s + a} $$

where \( G(s) \) is the transfer function, \( K \) is the system gain, and \( a \) is the damping factor.

Impact on Drone Control

Modern drones rely on **PID controllers** (Proportional-Integral-Derivative controllers) to stabilize their flight and respond to external disturbances effectively.
